Chicken Kolambu

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped onions and saute until they turn translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Cook until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the masala mixture.
  8. Add water to cover the chicken pieces and bring the mixture to a boil.
  9. Reduce the heat to flow, cover he pan, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender
  10. Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding more water if needed.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Tips

Marinate the chicken: Marinating the chicken in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and spices for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight, will enhance the flavor and tenderness of the chicken.

Use freshly ground spices: Grinding whole spices such as coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and fenugreek seeds just before adding them to the dish will release their essential oils and provide a more aromatic and flavorful kolambu.

Balance the flavours: Achieve a perfect balance of flavors by adjusting the amount of spice, tanginess, and saltiness to suit your taste preferences. Taste the kolambu as it cooks and make adjustments as needed to ensure a well-balanced and delicious dish.
