Chicken Briyani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaves)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon briyani masala powder
  • salt to taste
  • ghee or oil
  • saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Directions

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate the chicken piecs with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, briyani masala powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot. Add the sliced onions and saute until golden brown.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and cook until its partially done.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, green chillies, coriander leaves, mint leaves, and whole spices. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the chicken is fully cooked.
  6. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Gently mix everything together.
  7. Add enoug water to cook the rice (approximately 4 cups). Season with salt if needed. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and cook until the rice is done and all the water is absorbed.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, you can sprinkle saffron milk over the top for added flavor and color. Garnish with fried onions if desired.
  9. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or salan (curry)

Tips

Marinate the chicen well: Allow the chicken to marinate in yogurt and spices for at least an hour, or preferably longer, to ensure it absorbs all the flavours. This step adds depth of flavour to the dish.

Layering is key: Layering the partially cooked rice over the cooked chicken masala creates layers of flavor. Ensure the rice is cooked just about 70-8-% before layering it over the chicken. This allows the rice to finish cooking with the steam from the chicken. Resulting in perfectly cooked grains.

Use Quality Ingredients: Quality ingredients, especially aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, and saffron, can greatly enhance the taste of your briyani. Fresh herbs like coriander and mint also add a burst of freshness to the dish. Don't skimp on these, as they play a crucial role in the overall flavor profile of the briyani.
