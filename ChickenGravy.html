Chicken Gravy

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Water or chicken stock as needed
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Directions

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and saute until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute until the raw smell disappears
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes until the spices are fragrant.
  6. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the masala mixture.
  7. Add water or chicken stock to cover the chicken pieces. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender, stirring occasionally.
  9. Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding more water if needed.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  11. Serve hot with rice, roti, naan, or any bread of your chocie.

Tips

Use bone-in chicken: Using bone-in chicken pieces, such as thighs or drumsticks, adds depth of flavor to the gravy. The bones releasegelatin as they cook, Resulting in a richer and more flavorful sauce.

Saute the spices: Toasting whole spices like cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and cardamom pods in oil or ghee before adding other ingredients can enhance the aroma and flavor of the gravy. Be careful not to burn them, as it can make the gravy bitter.

Incorporate aromatics: Adding aromatic ingredients like onions, garlic, and ginger at the beginning of cooking creates a flavorful base for the gravy. Saute them until they are soft and golden brown to enhance their sweetness and depth of flavor.
